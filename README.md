# Identification of the "Basal Type" Endometrial Cancer

**A Novel Progenitor-Driven Subtype for Precision Stratification and Targeted Therapy**

*AACR Poster — Masahito Kawazu, M.D., Ph.D.*

---

## Overview

Endometrial cancer classification has evolved with TCGA molecular profiling, yet the **NSMP (no specific molecular profile)** group remains a "black box" lacking clear therapeutic targets.

Our group has identified a novel cell population — **SOX9 basal-like cells** — and a corresponding **"Basal type"** tumor that defines a distinct biological and clinical entity.

---

## Key Findings

### 🔬 Novel Diagnostic Biomarker
- Identified **N-cadherin (CDH2)** as a specific cell-surface marker for basal-type tumors
- Provides a robust axis for **patient stratification**


### 🎯 Targetable Biology
- Basal type tumors exhibit hyperactivation of **WNT/β-catenin** and other candidate oncogenic signaling pathways
- N-cadherin may serve as a co-receptor amplifying downstream oncogenic signaling, suggesting its potential as a target for **ADCs or therapeutic antibodies**
- Candidate pathways have been identified and are functionally addressable through our PDO platform

### 🧫 Established R&D Platform
- High-fidelity **Patient-Derived Organoid (PDO)** and **PDOX library** covering both Basal and non-Basal subtypes
- Platform ready for evaluating:
  - Novel inhibitors targeting identified candidate signaling pathways
  - Metabolic reprogramming agents
